About

Making complex systems work better for the people inside them.

Certified Lean Six Sigma Green Belt
MBA, Ateneo de Manila University
10+ years across 5 industries

Most growing businesses hit a wall. What worked at five people starts breaking at fifteen. Processes live in people's heads. Tools multiply without connecting. Everyone's busy, but things keep slipping through the cracks.

I come in, figure out where work is actually breaking down, and help fix it. Whether that means redesigning a process, writing an SOP, building an automation, or all three.

The goal is always the same: free your team up to focus on work that matters more.

And you don't have to be in chaos to benefit from this. If you're just starting out and want to build things right from the beginning, that's a great time to work together too. It's a lot easier to design a good process from scratch than to untangle a messy one later.

Services

Process improvement for businesses growing faster than their systems.

01

Process review and diagnosis

Not sure where things are breaking down? This is where we start. I sit with your team, map out how work actually flows, and surface the bottlenecks, gaps, and inefficiencies that are costing you time and money. You get a clear picture of what's going wrong and what to fix first.

Good for: teams that feel busy but stuck, or businesses preparing to scale.

02

Process redesign and documentation

Once we know what needs to change, we redesign it properly. Cleaner workflows, clearer ownership, and documented SOPs your team can actually follow. No more "it depends on who you ask" or processes that only work when a specific person is around.

Good for: teams where work is inconsistent, undocumented, or dependent on tribal knowledge.

03

Automation, AI workflows, and internal tools

When a process is ready to be automated, I build it. Workflows that run themselves, dashboards that update automatically, AI-assisted processes that help your team work faster. Not every process needs AI — but when it helps, I know how to apply it practically.

Good for: repetitive manual tasks, scattered data, or teams ready to scale without adding headcount.

04

AI basics and workflow training

A lot of people know AI is useful but have no idea where to start. I offer practical, jargon-free sessions for small groups — walking through what AI tools can actually do for your business and how to use them day to day. No theory, no hype. Just practical application.

Good for: business owners and small teams who want to use AI without feeling overwhelmed.

Work

A few things I've worked on.

01

From chat messages to a system everyone actually uses

Supply chain and operations, multi-brand team

+

The situation

The supply chain team was the go-to for three other departments, but there was no central place for requests to live. Teams sent chat messages, mentioned things in meetings, or followed up verbally. Everything was scattered. People had no visibility into where things stood unless someone remembered to follow up.

What I did

I designed and built a centralized intake system with a submission form and a tracking dashboard. The system handles production orders, inventory replenishment, barcode requests, and SKU code generation. The last one is fully agentic. Notifications go out automatically at the right points in the process.

The result

Four departments now use the system daily. Requests have a proper home. Anyone can check status without asking anyone. Manual group chat updates are gone. The supply chain team got back the time they were spending on follow-ups, and the requesting teams got the visibility they didn't have before.

02

Inventory data that updates itself, so your team doesn't have to

Amazon seller, 3 brands, 70+ SKUs

+

The situation

Knowing stock levels meant checking the Amazon seller account, vendor warehouse, production units, and multiple shipment stages. All of it pulled manually, across multiple brands, then consolidated into a spreadsheet. Multiple people spent recurring time on data that was outdated by the time they finished.

What I did

I built an automated dashboard pulling from all four data sources daily. Before building, I discovered data inconsistencies in one source and cleaned those up first. Getting the data right mattered more than the automation itself. Rollout was done one brand at a time to validate accuracy.

The result

A single dashboard that updates itself daily. What required multiple people and manual checks is now just there every morning. Recurring manual work removed from several people's plates. Inventory decisions now made on current data.

03

Turning a vision into a content pipeline the team actually relies on

Content and creative team

+

The situation

The content team needed a way to go from a trending TikTok video to a ready-to-use creative brief. An initial workflow existed but wasn't working reliably. Too many things were happening in one place. It needed significant rework before the team could actually use it.

What I did

I rebuilt it as three focused pipelines: video analysis using multiple AI models simultaneously, brief generation with defined content strategies and hooks, and brief delivery with automated notifications. Each pipeline does one thing well and feeds into the next.

The result

The pipeline is live and the team uses it. What started as a vision is now working infrastructure. Content creators get structured briefs based on real video data, without waiting on anyone to pull it together.

04

Leading a process improvement initiative across an entire organization

Multiple departments across a growing organization

+

The situation

As the organization grew, the way work actually happened became harder to see. Each department had developed its own way of doing things. Handoffs were unclear, and dependencies were invisible. There was no unified view of how work flowed, making it hard to spot risks before they became problems.

What I did

I led a structured, company-wide improvement initiative: sitting with process owners, mapping how work actually happens, identifying gaps and bottlenecks, and developing a concrete improvement plan for each department. Not everything needed automation. Some fixes were redesigns, guardrails, or clearer handoffs. I also coordinated an external consultant engagement with full knowledge transfer back internally.

The result

A clear, documented picture of operations across all departments. Process owners know what they own. Bottlenecks that had been invisible for a long time are now on the table. Each department has a concrete plan it can act on.

05

Improving how a global pharmaceutical supply chain documents and operates

Multinational pharmaceutical company, supply chain division

+

The situation

A global pharmaceutical supply chain had grown complex. Processes were documented inconsistently with no unified end-to-end picture. In a regulated environment, that isn't just an efficiency problem. It's a compliance risk.

What I did

Embedded in the team, I worked with subject matter experts to map end-to-end operations, then led the reconstruction of the division's SOPs, identifying inconsistencies, redundancies, and gaps across the full documentation body. I also led the process analysis workstream of a Lean Six Sigma project targeting a high-volume governing SOP.

The result

A cleaner, consistent set of SOPs reflecting how work actually flows. Process gaps and redundancies addressed. The improvement project delivered a verified 35% reduction in turnaround time.
